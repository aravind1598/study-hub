10 Mark Essay / Long Answer
1
Explain the basic organization of a computer with diagram.
▼
Basic Components:

1. Input Unit: Accepts data from user — Keyboard, Mouse, Scanner.
2. CPU (Central Processing Unit):
 • ALU (Arithmetic Logic Unit): Does calculations (+,-,×,÷) and comparisons.
 • CU (Control Unit): Controls all operations, directs data flow.
 • Registers: Temporary high-speed storage inside CPU.
3. Memory Unit:
 • Primary: RAM (temporary), ROM (permanent BIOS).
 • Secondary: Hard disk, SSD, Pen drive (permanent storage).
4. Output Unit: Displays results — Monitor, Printer, Speaker.

Input → CPU (CU + ALU + Registers) ↔ Memory → Output
Working: Input data is sent to CPU. CU controls ALU to process data. Results stored in memory, then sent to output device. ⭐ Most important 10 Mark

5 Mark
1
Explain all basic logic gates with truth tables.
▼
1. AND Gate: Y=A·B — Output 1 only when ALL inputs are 1.
2. OR Gate: Y=A+B — Output 1 when ANY input is 1.
3. NOT Gate: Y=A' — Inverts input. (Unary gate)
4. NAND Gate: Y=(A·B)' — AND then NOT. Universal gate.
5. NOR Gate: Y=(A+B)' — OR then NOT. Universal gate.
6. XOR Gate: Y=A⊕B — Output 1 when inputs are DIFFERENT.
7. XNOR Gate: Y=(A⊕B)' — Output 1 when inputs are SAME.
AND: 00→0, 01→0, 10→0, 11→1
OR: 00→0, 01→1, 10→1, 11→1
NOT: 0→1, 1→0
XOR: 00→0, 01→1, 10→1, 11→0
⭐ Very important — learn all gates!
10 Mark
1
Explain Boolean algebra laws and De Morgan's theorems with proofs.
▼
Boolean Algebra: Mathematical system for logic using 0 and 1.

Postulates:
• Variables: A, B, C... take values 0 or 1
• Operations: AND(·), OR(+), NOT(')

Laws of Boolean Algebra:
1. Identity: A+0=A | A·1=A
2. Null/Dominance: A+1=1 | A·0=0
3. Complement: A+A'=1 | A·A'=0
4. Idempotent: A+A=A | A·A=A
5. Involution: (A')'=A
6. Commutative: A+B=B+A | AB=BA
7. Associative: A+(B+C)=(A+B)+C
8. Distributive: A(B+C)=AB+AC
9. Absorption: A+AB=A | A(A+B)=A
De Morgan's Theorems:
Theorem 1: (A+B)' = A'·B' — Proved by truth table ✓
Theorem 2: (A·B)' = A'+B' — Proved by truth table ✓

Application: Used to simplify Boolean expressions and convert NAND/NOR circuits.

State and prove Binomial theorem for positive integer n.

1
Explain permutation and combination with all formulas and problems.
▼
Fundamental Principle of Counting:
If task A can be done in m ways and B in n ways, then A and B together = m×n ways.

Factorial: n! = n×(n-1)×...×2×1

Permutation: nPr = n!/(n-r)!
• All items: n! ways
• With repetition: nʳ ways
• Circular: (n-1)! ways

Combination: nCr = n!/(r!(n-r)!)
• nCr = nC(n-r)
• nC0 = nCn = 1

Probability:
P(A) = n(A)/n(S) where S = sample space
P(A') = 1-P(A)
P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)-P(A∩B)
For mutually exclusive: P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)

1
Derive all equations of motion. Explain projectile motion.
▼
Equations of Motion:
Consider body with initial velocity u, acceleration a, after time t:
1st: v = u + at (velocity-time relation)
2nd: s = ut + ½at² (position-time relation)
3rd: v² = u² + 2as (velocity-displacement)
Projectile Motion:
A body thrown at angle θ with horizontal:
• Horizontal motion: uniform velocity (ax=0)
  x = u·cosθ·t
• Vertical motion: uniform acceleration (ay=-g)
  y = u·sinθ·t - ½gt²

Time of flight: At highest point, vy=0 → t=usinθ/g; Total T=2usinθ/g
Max Height: H = u²sin²θ/2g
Range: R = u²sin2θ/g
Max Range at θ=45°: R_max = u²/g

State Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation.
▼
Every particle attracts every other particle with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

How does g vary with height?
▼
At height h above Earth's surface:
g_h = g(1 - 2h/R) when h << R
g_h = gR²/(R+h)² exact formula
g decreases as height increases.

Derive escape velocity.
▼
Escape velocity = minimum velocity needed to escape Earth's gravity.

KE of object = Gravitational PE
½mv² = GMm/R
v² = 2GM/R = 2gR (since GM=gR²)
v_e = √(2gR) = 11.2 km/s

1
Explain Kepler's laws of planetary motion.
▼
1st Law (Law of Orbits): All planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.

2nd Law (Law of Areas): The line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps equal areas in equal times. (Faster when closer to Sun)

3rd Law (Law of Periods): The square of the time period is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis.
T² ∝ a³ or T²/a³ = constant

1
ΔG < 0 means reaction is ___
▼
Spontaneous (proceeds on its own)
2
First law of thermodynamics: ΔU = ___
▼
ΔU = q + w (Heat absorbed + Work done ON system)
3
Gibbs equation: ΔG = ___
▼
ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
4
Entropy is measure of ___
▼
Disorder/Randomness in a system. Symbol: S.
5
Endothermic reaction: ΔH is ___
▼
Positive (+ve) — heat is absorbed from surroundings.
2 Mark
1
Differentiate exothermic and endothermic reactions.
▼
Exothermic: Heat is released. ΔH is negative. Example: Combustion, Respiration.
Endothermic: Heat is absorbed. ΔH is positive. Example: Photosynthesis, Dissolving NH₄Cl.
2
State 2nd law of thermodynamics.
▼
The entropy of the universe always increases in a spontaneous process.
ΔS_universe = ΔS_system + ΔS_surroundings ≥ 0
3 Mark
1
Explain Hess's Law with example.
▼
Hess's Law: The total enthalpy change is the same regardless of the path taken.

Example: C + O₂ → CO₂ (ΔH = -393 kJ)
This can be done in one step or two steps (via CO), the total ΔH is always -393 kJ.

Application: Calculate ΔH of reactions that cannot be directly measured.
5 Mark
1
Explain Gibbs free energy and predict spontaneity.
▼
Gibbs Free Energy: G = H - TS
Change: ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

Spontaneity prediction:
ΔG < 0 → Spontaneous (forward)
ΔG = 0 → Equilibrium
ΔG > 0 → Non-spontaneous (reverse)
4 Cases based on ΔH and ΔS:
1. ΔH(-), ΔS(+): Always spontaneous
2. ΔH(+), ΔS(-): Never spontaneous
3. ΔH(-), ΔS(-): Spontaneous at low T
4. ΔH(+), ΔS(+): Spontaneous at high T
⭐ 10 Mark often!
10 Mark
1
Explain laws of thermodynamics, enthalpy, entropy and Gibbs energy.
▼
Zeroth Law: If A is in equilibrium with B, and B with C, then A is in equilibrium with C. (Basis of temperature measurement)

First Law: Energy is conserved. ΔU = q + w
For constant pressure: ΔH = ΔU + PΔV = ΔU + ΔnRT

Second Law: Entropy of universe increases. ΔS_universe ≥ 0

Third Law: Entropy of perfect crystal = 0 at absolute zero (0K)

Enthalpy (H): Heat content at constant pressure.
ΔH = negative → exothermic; positive → endothermic

Entropy (S): Measure of disorder.
ΔS = qrev/T (at constant temperature)

Gibbs Energy: ΔG = ΔH - TΔS
Predicts spontaneity at constant T and P.

Ch 8 — Equilibrium & Ionic

Kc, Kp, pH scale, Le Chatelier

▼
1 Mark
1
pH of pure water = ___
▼
7 (neutral)
2
pH + pOH = ___
▼
14 (at 25°C)
3
Le Chatelier's principle: If pressure is increased, equilibrium shifts towards ___
▼
fewer moles of gas side
4
Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn. What is Δn?
▼
Δn = (moles of gaseous products) - (moles of gaseous reactants)
2 Mark
1
Define pH. Calculate pH if [H⁺] = 10⁻³ M.
▼
pH = -log[H⁺]
= -log(10⁻³) = 3
Since pH < 7, it is acidic.
2
State Le Chatelier's principle.
▼
If a system at equilibrium is subjected to a change, the system adjusts itself to oppose the change and restore equilibrium.
Changes: temperature, pressure, concentration.
3 Mark
1
Explain relationship between Kc and Kp.
▼
For gaseous equilibrium:
Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn
Where R = 0.0821 L·atm/mol·K, T = temperature in Kelvin
Δn = moles of gaseous products - moles of gaseous reactants

• If Δn = 0: Kp = Kc
• If Δn > 0: Kp > Kc
• If Δn < 0: Kp < Kc
5 Mark
1
Explain pH scale and buffer solutions.
▼
pH Scale (0 to 14):
pH < 7 → Acidic (more H⁺)
pH = 7 → Neutral
pH > 7 → Basic/Alkaline (more OH⁻)
pH = -log[H⁺] | pOH = -log[OH⁻]
pH + pOH = 14

Strong acid: HCl, H₂SO₄ (fully ionize)
Weak acid: CH₃COOH (partially ionize)

Buffer Solution: Resists change in pH.
Acidic buffer: Weak acid + its salt (CH₃COOH + CH₃COONa)
Basic buffer: Weak base + its salt (NH₄OH + NH₄Cl)
10 Mark
1
Explain chemical equilibrium, Le Chatelier's principle with applications.
▼
Chemical Equilibrium: State where rate of forward reaction = rate of backward reaction. Concentrations remain constant.

Equilibrium Constant Kc:
For: aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD
Kc = [C]^c[D]^d / [A]^a[B]^b

Le Chatelier's Principle: Stress → system shifts to minimize it.
1. ↑ Concentration of reactant → shift right (more products)
2. ↑ Pressure → shift to side with fewer gas moles
3. ↑ Temperature → shift towards endothermic direction

Application — Haber's Process (NH₃):
N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (ΔH = -92 kJ)
• High pressure: shifts right ✓
• Low temperature: favors product, but slows rate
• Compromise: 500°C, 200 atm, Fe catalyst

2 Mark
1
Change to passive: "She writes a letter."
▼
A letter is written by her.
Rule: Object → Subject | is/am/are + V3 | Subject → by + Object
2
Change to indirect speech: He said, "I am happy."
▼
He said that he was happy.
Changes: "I" → "he", "am" → "was", commas/quotes removed.
3
Write all 3 types of conditionals.
▼
Type 1 (Real): If + present simple, will + V1
If it rains, I will stay home.
Type 2 (Unreal Present): If + past simple, would + V1
If I had money, I would buy a car.
Type 3 (Unreal Past): If + past perfect, would have + V3
If I had studied, I would have passed.
3 Mark
1
Explain tense shift in indirect speech.
▼
Direct → Indirect (tense shift):
is/are → was/were
was/were → had been
will → would
can → could
may → might
shall → should
have → had
do/does → did
Time expressions: now→then | today→that day | here→there | tomorrow→next day
2
Explain passive voice formation for all tenses.
▼
Simple Present: am/is/are + V3
Simple Past: was/were + V3
Future: will be + V3
Present Cont: am/is/are being + V3
Past Cont: was/were being + V3
Present Perfect: has/have been + V3
Past Perfect: had been + V3
